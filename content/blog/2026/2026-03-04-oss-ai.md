<img src="https://r2.duti.dev/blog/images/go-micro-commits.png" alt="Commit history of go-micro with Copilot commits" height="400">

At first glance, this looks like the commit history of just another random vibe
coded project. But no, it is in fact the recent commits to
[`go-micro`](https://github.com/micro/go-micro), a project with 22.7k stars that
started all the way back in 2015.
[BcacheFS](https://github.com/koverstreet/bcachefs), a major filesystem, now has
`ProofOfConcept` (a custom AI bot) as co-author on the majority of new commits.
The [Claude account](https://github.com/claude) on GitHub now has so many
commits that its GitHub profile fails to even load.

This worries me.

When it's Claude code
[randomly creating 10GB VM bundles](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/22543),
we point and laugh. When
[Cline gets supply chain attacked because of a prompt injection](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/cline-cli-230-supply-chain-attack.html),
nobody cares because dependence on them is purely individual. Very few build on
top of those shaky towers.

But now AI is coming for the foundations of what we build. I personally don't
have much confidence in either the quality or security of what AI pumps out.

[`chardet`](https://github.com/chardet/chardet/issues/327) recently got
completely rewritten by AI as a means of license washing (getting rid of LGPL)
while breaking various behavior and generally making the quality worse. Again,
not a small package. It is a dependency of almost 1 million packages tracked by
GitHub.

I don't even know what I want to say yet, other than that I do not like this new
iteration of open-source and I believe this behavior will become more widespread
over time. Barley anyone gets paid for it, and LLMs are an easy way out of
spending the effort maintaining a library.
